# Lesson-w02-1

## Primer

Two small declensions finish the noun system: the 4th (senatus, manus, exercitus — a
handful of nouns, several of them central to our political vocabulary) and the 5th
(res, dies — tiny, but res publica alone justifies it). After today, every noun in
every reading is one of five patterns you know. The trap to arrive aware of: 4th
declension -us forms are everywhere ambiguous (nom. sg., gen. sg., nom./acc. pl.), so
context and agreement carry more weight than usual.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: the last two noun patterns, and the -us ambiguity.

1. **Hook (4 min).** `senatus consultum` — he's met senatus a dozen times in week 1
   readings. Ask him to decline it cold. Where it wobbles, that's today's work.
2. **Fourth declension (10 min).** exercitus paradigm; the -us/-us/-ui/-um/-u song;
   gen. sg. -ūs vs nom. pl. -ūs — resolve by agreement (hic senatus / huius senatus).
   manus is feminine; domus is a hybrid (note domi "at home", domum "homeward",
   domo "from home" — locative-flavored idioms worth one minute now, they're frequent).
   cornu (n.) mentioned for completeness only.
3. **Fifth declension (8 min).** res, dies full paradigms — that's nearly the whole
   declension. res idioms tour: res publica, res novae, res gestae, re vera. dies
   gender note (m., but f. for appointed days — recognition only).
4. **Ambiguity drill (5 min).** Parse in context: `exercitus venit` / `exercitus
   ducis` / `exercitus vidi` — same form, three cases; what disambiguates each?
5. **Wrap (3 min).** Homework (~25 min). Next lesson: pronouns — the words that stitch
   sentences into stories.
