# Lesson-w02-1 — Reference: Declensions 4 and 5

## Fourth declension — exercitus, exercitūs (m.)

| | sg. | pl. |
|---|---|---|
| nom. | exercitus | exercitūs |
| gen. | exercitūs | exercituum |
| dat. | exercituī | exercitibus |
| acc. | exercitum | exercitūs |
| abl. | exercitū | exercitibus |

- Most are masculine. **manus is feminine** (magna manus). So is domus.
- The -us ambiguity: exercitus = nom. sg. OR gen. sg. OR nom./acc. pl. (vowel length
  distinguishes in vocab lists: -us vs -ūs; readings won't show it). Resolve by
  agreement and verb number.
- Our 4th-decl. regulars: senātus, cōnsulātus, magistrātus, exercitus, manus, domus,
  impetus, metus, tumultus.

## domus — the hybrid (4th/2nd) with locative idioms

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| domī | at home |
| domum | (to) home, homeward |
| domō | from home |

Otherwise mostly 4th-declension forms; abl. sg. domō, acc. pl. domōs are the common
2nd-declension crossovers. Recognition only.

## Fifth declension — rēs, reī (f.) and diēs, diēī (m.)

| | rēs sg. | rēs pl. | diēs sg. | diēs pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| nom. | rēs | rēs | diēs | diēs |
| gen. | reī | rērum | diēī | diērum |
| dat. | reī | rēbus | diēī | diēbus |
| acc. | rem | rēs | diem | diēs |
| abl. | rē | rēbus | diē | diēbus |

- Genitive/dative -ēī after a vowel (diēī), -eī after a consonant (reī, speī, fideī).
- diēs is masculine; feminine when it means a fixed/appointed day (recognition only).
- Nearly all 5th-decl. nouns you will ever meet: rēs, diēs, spēs, fidēs, aciēs.

## rēs — one noun, a political vocabulary

| Idiom | Meaning |
|---|---|
| rēs pūblica | the republic, the state ("the public thing") |
| rēs novae | revolution ("new things") |
| rēs gestae | deeds, achievements |
| rē vērā | in fact, really |
| rēs mīlitāris | warfare, the art of war |
