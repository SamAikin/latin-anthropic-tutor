# Lesson-w01-4 — Reference: Prepositions and Case Idioms

## Prepositions with the accusative (motion toward/through/against, extent)

| Prep. | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ad | to, toward; at | ad forum venit |
| in (+acc) | into, onto, against | in urbem, in hostēs |
| per | through, throughout; by means of | per Ītaliam |
| trāns | across | trāns flūmen |
| inter | between, among | inter cīvēs |
| ante / post | before / after, behind | ante portās / post bellum |
| ob / propter | on account of | propter metum |
| contrā | against | contrā lēgēs |
| apud | at the house of, among, in the works of | apud senātum |

## Prepositions with the ablative (location, source, accompaniment)

| Prep. | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| in (+abl) | in, on | in forō |
| ā / ab | from, away from; BY (agent, with passive) | ā mīlitibus |
| ē / ex | out of, from | ex agrīs |
| dē | down from; about, concerning | dē bellō dīcere |
| cum | with (accompaniment, manner) | cum amīcīs |
| sine | without | sine armīs |
| prō | in front of; on behalf of; instead of | prō patriā |

in and sub take BOTH cases: accusative = motion (in urbem, into the city), ablative =
location (in urbe, in the city).

## Bare-case idioms (no preposition — English adds one)

| Idiom | Case | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| time when | ablative | illō diē, prīmā lūce | on that day, at dawn |
| time within | ablative | tribus annīs | within three years |
| time duration | accusative | multōs annōs | for many years |
| means/instrument | ablative | ferrō, suffrāgiīs | by the sword, by votes |
| manner | cum + abl., or bare abl. + adj. | cum cūrā / magnā cūrā | with care |
| agent (passive only) | ā/ab + ablative | ā cōnsule | by the consul |

**The agent/means distinction:** a person acting = ā/ab + abl. (ā mīlite occīsus est);
a thing used = bare ablative (gladiō occīsus est). Readings lean on this constantly.

## From the diagnostic, corrected and filed

- `Cōnsul in senātū dē bellō dīcēbat.` — in + abl: he was speaking IN the senate
  (location), about the war (dē + abl). Not "telling the senate."
