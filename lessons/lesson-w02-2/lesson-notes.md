# Lesson-w02-2

## Primer

Pronouns are how Latin prose stitches sentences into narrative — and this week's
readings (the Gracchi, Marius, Sulla) lean on them hard: hic vir, ille Marius, eadem
via, ipse consul. You already read qui-clauses well. Today systematizes the
demonstratives and intensifiers: is (the workhorse "he/she/it/that"), hic/ille (near/
far — and in historical prose, "the latter/the former"), iste (that-of-yours, often
with a sneer — Cicero's favorite weapon), ipse (-self, the emphasizer), idem (the
same). The reading skill underneath: every pronoun points somewhere; always know its
antecedent, and its gender/number will confirm it.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: the demonstrative system and antecedent-tracking.

1. **Hook (4 min).** From this week's reading w02-2: `Hic vir maiora consilia habebat
   quam ille.` Two brothers are in play — who is hic, who is ille, and what rule
   decides? (Near/far in the discourse: hic = the one just introduced, Gaius.)
2. **Paradigm cores (10 min).** is/ea/id in full (it's the skeleton for the rest);
   hic and ille nominatives + the -ius genitive / -i dative pattern all these share
   (huius, illius, ipsius, eiusdem... unius, solius, totius from week 2 vocab too —
   one pattern, many words). Recognition-first for oblique forms.
3. **Meaning work (12 min).** iste + contempt (preview: Cicero will aim `iste` at
   Catiline in week 4's reading — plant the flag). ipse vs. an intensified is
   (`consul ipse venit` vs `eum vidi`). idem for "the same" (`eadem verba`). Then
   antecedent drills on mini-discourses: two sentences, several pronouns, he maps
   each pronoun to its noun and justifies by gender/number.
4. **Wrap (4 min).** Homework (~30 min). Next lesson: the passive voice completes
   the verb picture for tense; then deponents close the week.
