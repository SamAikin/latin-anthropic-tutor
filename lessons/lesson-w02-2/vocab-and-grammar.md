## hic and ille — near and far

| | hic m. | haec f. | hoc n. | ille m. | illa f. | illud n. |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| nom. sg. | hic | haec | hoc | ille | illa | illud |
| gen. sg. | huius | huius | huius | illīus | illīus | illīus |
| dat. sg. | huic | huic | huic | illī | illī | illī |
| acc. sg. | hunc | hanc | hoc | illum | illam | illud |
| abl. sg. | hōc | hāc | hōc | illō | illā | illō |

Plurals follow the is/ea/id pattern (hī, hae, haec; illī, illae, illa...).

- hic = this (near me / just mentioned); ille = that (far / mentioned earlier); in
  paired use: hic "the latter," ille "the former."
- ille also = "the famous": `ille Marius`.

## The shared irregular pattern: gen. -īus, dat. -ī

One pattern runs through many words: **huius/huic, illīus/illī, istīus/istī,
ipsīus/ipsī, eius/eī, eiusdem/eīdem — and the adjectives ūnus, sōlus, tōtus, alter,
uter, neuter, nūllus, ūllus (ūnīus/ūnī, sōlīus/sōlī, tōtīus/tōtī...).** Learn the
pattern once; recognize it everywhere.

## iste, ipse, īdem — the flavor pronouns

- **iste, ista, istud** (declines like ille): that-of-yours; in oratory, usually
  contemptuous — `iste furor tuus`. When Cicero says iste, someone is in trouble.
- **ipse, ipsa, ipsum** (gen. ipsīus): -self, the very. Intensifies, doesn't
  reflexivize: `consul ipse` = the consul himself. (Contrast sē, the reflexive object:
  `sē laudat` = he praises himself.)
- **īdem, eadem, idem** (= is + -dem): the same. `eadem verba`, `eōdem tempore`.

## Reading strategy: antecedent tracking

Every pronoun points at something. On meeting one: (1) find the candidate noun in the
previous clause or sentence; (2) check gender and number match; (3) if two candidates
match, hic points to the nearer/later one, ille to the farther/earlier one. Never let
a pronoun float unattached.
