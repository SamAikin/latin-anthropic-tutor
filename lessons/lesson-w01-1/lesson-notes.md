# Lesson-w01-1

## Primer

This lesson restarts the engine: the case system, using declensions 1 and 2. You
already read case functions well (the diagnostic showed nominative, accusative, dative,
and ablative-with-preposition all working), so this is consolidation at speed, not
first contact. The two corners the diagnostic never probed — the genitive and the
vocative — get deliberate attention. The frame to hold: **Latin endings do the work
English word order does.** Before the lesson, glance at the paradigm tables in this
folder's vocab-and-grammar.md; don't memorize, just reacquaint.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: case = role, independent of position.

1. **Hook (5 min).** Show `Agricola filium vocat` / `Filium agricola vocat` / `Vocat
   filium agricola` — same meaning three times. Socratic: what tells you who calls
   whom? Draw out: the endings, and only the endings.
2. **Paradigms at speed (10 min).** Walk decl 1 (puella), decl 2 masc (dominus, puer,
   ager), decl 2 neut (bellum) from the Reference tables. Have him produce forms, not
   recite tables: "genitive plural of bellum?" Check the neuter rule (nom = acc,
   plural in -a) explicitly.
3. **The two rusty corners (10 min).** Genitive: possession and the of-relationship
   (`consilium senatus`, `pars urbis`); have him build phrases. Vocative: address,
   = nominative except decl 2 -e/-i (`Marce!`, `fili!`); why it matters for reading
   speeches. Rapid-fire: give six forms, he names case + function in a sentence.
4. **Wrap (5 min).** Preview homework (in homework.md — ~25 min). Note: tomorrow's
   lesson adds declension 3, where the same case logic meets less familiar endings.

Socratic throughout — he demonstrates, you confirm. Do NOT drill sentences that mirror
homework items (no spoilers). If the genitive is instantly solid, bank the time and
probe vocative + apposition instead.

## Homework

Complete homework.md in this folder (~25 min): one declension write-out, case
identification in context, and five short translations. No reference materials during
the translation section; the declension write-out may be checked against the tables
afterward, before you turn it in — mark anything you had to correct.
