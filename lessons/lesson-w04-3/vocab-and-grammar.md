# Lesson-w04-3 — Reference: Conditions (Recognition-First)

## The three families

| Family | Verb forms | Example | English frame |
|---|---|---|---|
| REAL | indicative, any tense | Sī hoc dīcit, errat. | If he says this, he IS wrong. |
| FUTURE | future / future perfect indicative | Sī vēnerit, eum vidēbō. | If he comes, I WILL see him. |
| CONTRARY-TO-FACT | subjunctive | see below | WOULD / WOULD HAVE |

Real and future conditions: you already read every form involved. Only the third
family is new — and only for recognition this block.

## Contrary-to-fact: the two silhouettes

**Present unreal — imperfect subjunctive.** Silhouette: looks like the PRESENT
INFINITIVE with personal endings.

- amāret (amāre + t), dīceret, adesset, sequerētur
- `Sī Cicerō adesset, aliter loquerētur.` — If Cicero WERE here (he isn't), he WOULD
  be speaking differently.

**Past unreal — pluperfect subjunctive.** Silhouette: the -isse- core (same as the
perfect infinitive) with personal endings.

- amāvisset (amāvisse + t), scīvisset, vēnisset, cēpisset
- `Sī hoc scīvissem, numquam vēnissem.` — If I HAD known this, I WOULD never HAVE
  come. (The diagnostic sentence.)

Recognition rule of thumb: **infinitive-shape + ending = would; -isse- + ending =
would have.** That heuristic covers the conditions in all four weeks of readings and
most of what Cicero will throw at you next block.

## Sorting procedure

1. See sī (or nisi = if-not/unless).
2. Look at the verbs on BOTH sides.
3. Indicative → real. Future/future-perfect → future ("if he comes...").
   Infinitive-shape or -isse- shape → contrary-to-fact; pick would/would-have by
   silhouette.

## Specimens

- `Sī hostēs vēnerint, mīlitēs nostrī urbem dēfendent.` — future (vēnerit = fut.
  perf.: "if the enemy [will have] come...")
- `Sī coniūrātiōnem nōn cognōvisset, rēs pūblica cecidisset.` (reading w04-6) — past
  contrafactual: had he not discovered... it would have fallen.
- `Nisi Atticus cautus fuisset, cum Sulpiciō periisset.` — unless-shape, past unreal.
