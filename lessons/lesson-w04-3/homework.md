## Exercises or Reading

**A. Sort each condition into its family (REAL / FUTURE / CONTRARY-TO-FACT present /
CONTRARY-TO-FACT past), then translate:**

1. Si consul hoc dicit, verum dicit.
2. Si milites venerint, hostes fugient.
3. Si Gracchus viveret, plebs ducem haberet.
4. Si senatus citius egisset, Gracchus non periisset.
5. Nisi Sulla urbem cepisset, alii idem fecissent? — sort it, translate it, then one
   sentence in English: do you buy the claim? (This is a history question hiding in a
   grammar exercise.)
6. Si leges non servantur, res publica non stat.

**B. Translate (mixed conditions in context):**

1. Si Cicero coniurationem non cognovisset, coniurati urbem incendissent.
2. Si populus novos consules creaverit, nobiles eos non amabunt.
3. Si Atticus in Italia mansisset, alterutram partem offendisset.
4. Nisi milites duci fideles essent, nemo contra rem publicam arma ferret.

**C. One diagnostic, revisited.** Translate precisely, then in one sentence explain
what your diagnostic answer got right and what it missed:

Si hoc scivissem, numquam venissem.
