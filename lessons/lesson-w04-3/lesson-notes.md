# Lesson-w04-3

## Primer

The diagnostic's last casualty: `Si hoc scivissem, numquam venissem` — you sensed a
conditional but lost the timeline ("If I knew that, I would fear nothing"). It means
"If I had known this, I would never have COME" — a past contrafactual, and both verbs
are pluperfect **subjunctive**. Today is deliberately recognition-first: the full
subjunctive system is next block's centerpiece, so we are NOT learning subjunctive
morphology tables. We are learning to recognize the three condition families on
sight — real, future, contrary-to-fact — because they're common in every author and
because your diagnostic says the contrafactual timeline is exactly where you slip.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: the three condition families, by silhouette.

1. **Hook (5 min).** The diagnostic sentence, dissected: scivissem/venissem — the
   -isse- core (like the perfect infinitive!) + personal endings = pluperfect
   subjunctive silhouette. Meaning: past-and-unreal. "Had known... would never have
   come." His diagnostic answer beside it: the structure he sensed, the timeline he
   lost. Today fixes the timeline.
2. **The three families (12 min).**
   - REAL (indicative both sides): `Si hoc dicit, errat.` If he says this, he's wrong.
     Nothing new — he's been reading these for weeks.
   - FUTURE (future/future-perfect indicative): `Si venerit, eum videbo.` — futurum
     perfect in the if-clause, familiar from w01-3.
   - CONTRARY-TO-FACT (subjunctive): present unreal = imperfect subj. (`si adesset,
     iuvaret` — silhouette: infinitive + endings); past unreal = pluperfect subj.
     (`si adfuisset, iuvisset` — silhouette: -isse- + endings).
   Recognition anchors only: "infinitive-plus-endings" and "-isse-plus-endings."
3. **Silhouette drills (8 min).** Eight if-sentences, he sorts by family and gives
   the English frame (does/will/would/would-have). No production, no paradigms —
   sorting and translating only.
4. **Wrap (5 min).** Where he'll meet these: Cicero argues in contrafactuals
   constantly ("had I acted sooner, no one would have died" — the shape of In Cat.);
   reading w04-6 closes on one. Homework (~25 min). Tomorrow: block synthesis —
   sight-reading everything at once.
