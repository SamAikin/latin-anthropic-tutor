# Lesson-w01-2 — Reference: Declension 3 and Adjective Agreement

## Third declension — rēx, rēgis (m.)

| | sg. | pl. |
|---|---|---|
| nom. | rēx | rēgēs |
| gen. | rēgis | rēgum |
| dat. | rēgī | rēgibus |
| acc. | rēgem | rēgēs |
| abl. | rēge | rēgibus |

The nominative is unpredictable (rēx, homō, mīles, cīvitās…); **the genitive singular
gives the stem**: rēg-, homin-, mīlit-, cīvitāt-. This is why dictionary form includes
the genitive.

## Third declension neuter — tempus, temporis (n.)

| | sg. | pl. |
|---|---|---|
| nom. | tempus | tempora |
| gen. | temporis | temporum |
| dat. | temporī | temporibus |
| acc. | tempus | tempora |
| abl. | tempore | temporibus |

Neuter rule holds: nom = acc, plural -a.

## I-stems — urbs, urbis (f.) and mare, maris (n.)

Practical reading rules (recognition-first; don't memorize the classification criteria):

- gen. pl. in **-ium**: urbium, cīvium, hostium, partium, montium
- neuters in -e, -al, -ar: abl. sg. **-ī** (marī), nom./acc. pl. **-ia** (maria)

Common i-stems in our vocab: urbs, cīvis, hostis, pars, mors, nāvis, classis, mare.

## Adjective agreement

Agreement = same **case, number, gender**. NOT same ending.

**2-1-2 adjectives** (magnus, magna, magnum) use decl 1–2 endings, whatever the noun:

- magnus rēx, magnī rēgis, magnō rēgī — decl-2 endings on the adjective, decl-3 on the noun
- magna pars, magnae partis…

**Third-declension adjectives** use decl-3 i-stem endings (abl. sg. -ī, gen. pl. -ium):

| | m./f. sg. | n. sg. | m./f. pl. | n. pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| nom. | fortis | forte | fortēs | fortia |
| gen. | fortis | fortis | fortium | fortium |
| dat. | fortī | fortī | fortibus | fortibus |
| acc. | fortem | forte | fortēs | fortia |
| abl. | fortī | fortī | fortibus | fortibus |

One-termination type: ingēns (gen. ingentis) — same pattern, one nominative for all
genders. Cross-declension pairs to internalize the principle:

- fortis puella (decl-3 adj + decl-1 noun)
- magnō homini (decl-2 adj + decl-3 noun)
- omnium bellōrum (decl-3 adj + decl-2 noun)
