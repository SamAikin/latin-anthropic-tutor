# Lesson-w04-4

## Primer

Block synthesis. No new grammar today — instead, proof of work: three short passages
you have never seen, read at sight in the lesson, combining everything from four
weeks (all five declensions, full indicative both voices, deponents, participles,
ablative absolutes, infinitives, indirect statement, and one condition). Then a block
retrospective: what runs smooth, what still costs effort. Be honest in the
retrospective — it is the primary input for planning block 2, and overclaiming
comfort buys you a block that fights you.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: integration under mild pressure.

1. **Sight-reading (18 min).** Three passages from the Reference below, unseen until
   the lesson. Protocol per passage: he reads silently (~1 min), then translates
   aloud, flagging uncertainty as he goes (his diagnostic self-flagging was accurate —
   trust it). Tutor notes, per passage: constructions handled cold vs. hesitations
   vs. misses. NO teaching interruptions during a passage; debrief 1–2 min after
   each.
2. **Retrospective dialogue (8 min).** Four questions, one at a time:
   - Which construction still requires conscious effort? (predicted: abl. abs. vs
     attached participle discrimination, or relative tense in indirect statement)
   - Which readings this block felt best / worst, and why?
   - Vocab: which week's words stuck hardest? Anki retention feel?
   - Pace check: was the speed run right — where did it under- or over-shoot?
3. **Wrap (4 min).** Homework is light (~15 min): written block self-assessment —
   the same four questions, in writing, plus one free-form note to the planner
   (me, next session). Note what comes next: block 2 planning session (plan-syllabus),
   expected centerpiece: the subjunctive system.

**Passage 2 (indirect statement, Nepos-flavored):**
Atticus amicis dicebat se nullam partem secuturum esse. Putabat enim eos, qui se
civilibus bellis dederant, non magis sua consilia regere quam navem in magna
tempestate. Itaque Athenas profectus est; omnes intellexerunt eum sapientem esse.

**Passage 3 (mixed, with one condition):**
Cicero dixit se coniurationem cognovisse et litteras coniuratorum senatui ostendit.
Si consul tacuisset, urbs incensa esset; sed oratione habita, patres omnia
intellexerunt. Catilina, ex urbe pulsus, ad exercitum suum profectus est.
