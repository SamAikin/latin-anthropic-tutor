# Lesson-w01-3 — Reference: The Active Indicative System

## Principal parts = the machine

capiō, capere, cēpī, captum

| Part | Form | Factory for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | capiō | present-system endings, 1st person |
| 2 | capere | conjugation class; present stem |
| 3 | cēpī | the entire perfect system |
| 4 | captum | participles, passive perfect system (week 3) |

## The six tenses, two systems

**Present system** (present stem):

| Tense | Signal | dūcō (3rd sg.) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| present | — | dūcit | leads, is leading |
| imperfect | -bā- | dūcēbat | was leading, used to lead |
| future | -bi- (1st/2nd conj.), -ē- (3rd/4th) | dūcet | will lead |

Future watch-out: 1st/2nd conj. use -bi- (vocābit, tenēbit); 3rd/4th use -ē-/-iē-
(dūcet, capiet, veniet). A 3rd-conj. "ducet" looks like a 2nd-conj. present — the
dictionary form disambiguates.

**Perfect system** (perfect stem, same endings for ALL verbs):

| Tense | Signal | dūcō (3rd sg.) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| perfect | -ī, -istī, -it… | dūxit | led, has led |
| pluperfect | -erā- | dūxerat | had led |
| future perfect | -eri- | dūxerit | will have led |

Perfect endings: -ī, -istī, -it, -imus, -istis, -ērunt.

## Conjugation classes at a glance

| Conj. | Infinitive | Present 3rd sg. | Future 3rd sg. |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | -āre (vocāre) | vocat | vocābit |
| 2nd | -ēre (tenēre) | tenet | tenēbit |
| 3rd | -ere (dūcere) | dūcit | dūcet |
| 3rd -iō | -ere (capere) | capit | capiet |
| 4th | -īre (venīre) | venit | veniet |

## Common irregulars — recognition table

| | present | imperfect | future | perfect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| sum | est | erat | erit | fuit |
| possum | potest | poterat | poterit | potuit |
| ferō | fert | ferēbat | feret | tulit |
| volō | vult | volēbat | volet | voluit |
| eō | it | ībat | ībit | iit |

Note tulī: the perfect of ferō comes from a different root entirely. When a perfect
form seems to match no verb you know, suspect an irregular and check principal parts.
