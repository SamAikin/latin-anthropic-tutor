## Exercises or Reading

**A. Attached or absolute? Then translate:**

1. Urbe capta, milites praedam petebant.
2. Urbem captam milites incenderunt.
3. Signo dato, equites impetum fecerunt.
4. Litteras a Caesare missas consul legit.
5. Consule occiso, nemo leges servabat.
6. Milites ducem multa pollicentem audiebant.

**B. Translate (mixed variants — perfect, present, and no-participle types):**

1. Hostibus victis, exercitus Romam rediit.
2. Caesare veniente, Helvetii legatos miserunt.
3. Cicerone consule, coniuratio Catilinae patefacta est.
4. Militibus flumen transeuntibus, hostes impetum fecerunt.
5. Pace facta, captivi domum redierunt.
6. Sulla dictatore, multi cives sine iudicio perierunt.

**C. Compose in Latin using an ablative absolute (short):**

1. After the letters were read, the senate was silent. (use: litterae, lego / senatus,
   taceo)
2. With Marius as leader, the soldiers feared nothing. (no-participle type)
