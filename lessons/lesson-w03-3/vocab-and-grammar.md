# Lesson-w03-3 — Reference: The Ablative Absolute

## The pattern

**Noun + participle, both ablative, grammatically detached from the main clause.**

`Urbe capta, cives fugerunt.`
- urbe capta: ablative + ablative, agreeing
- the main clause (cives fugerunt) is complete without it
- the noun (urbe) plays NO role in the main clause — that detachment is what makes it
  "absolute"

Contrast (yesterday's attached sandwich): `Urbem captam incenderunt` — urbem IS the
object of incenderunt. Attached = some case, playing a role. Absolute = ablative,
playing none.

## The translation menu

The construction states circumstance; context picks the English connective:

| Flavor | `Urbe capta, cives fugerunt.` |
|---|---|
| temporal | AFTER the city was taken, the citizens fled |
| causal | BECAUSE the city had been taken... |
| concessive | ALTHOUGH the city had been taken... (rarer; context signals it) |
| plain | WITH the city taken... |

The literal scaffold — "the city having-been-captured" — is for construction-checking
only; never leave it in a translation.

## Tense inside the absolute (relative, as always)

- Perfect participle: action BEFORE the main verb — `urbe capta, fugerunt` (taken
  first, fled after)
- Present participle: SAME time — `Caesare veniente, hostes discesserunt` (as Caesar
  was coming)

## The no-participle type (dating formula — everywhere in real texts)

Latin has no present participle of esse, so noun + noun/adjective stands alone:

- `Cicerone consule` — with Cicero (being) consul / in Cicero's consulship
- `Sulla dictatore` — under Sulla's dictatorship
- `me invito` — against my will

## Real specimens from this week's Eutropius (reading w03-5)

- `effracto Capuae ludo fugerunt` — the school at Capua having been broken open,
  they fled → they broke out of the school at Capua and fled
- `multis ducibus et duobus simul consulibus victis` — many commanders and two
  consuls at once having been defeated → after defeating many commanders and two
  consuls at once

Note what Eutropius packs into one absolute: an entire military campaign, backgrounded
in six words, so the main clause can carry the new information. That economy is WHY
the construction exists.
