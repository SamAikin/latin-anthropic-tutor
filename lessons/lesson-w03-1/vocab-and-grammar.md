# Lesson-w03-1 — Reference: The Participle System

## The grid (learn the ADDRESSES, manufacture the forms)

| | Active | Passive |
|---|---|---|
| Present | capiēns, -entis — "taking" | — (doesn't exist) |
| Perfect | — (doesn't exist)* | captus, -a, -um — "(having been) taken" |
| Future | captūrus, -a, -um — "about to take" | capiendus (gerundive — parked for a later block) |

*Deponents fill the gap: their perfect participle is ACTIVE in meaning — secūtus =
"having followed." This is the only way Latin says "having X-ed" in one word, and
authors exploit it constantly.

## Manufacturing instructions

| Participle | Built from | Recipe | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| present active | present stem | + -ns, gen. -ntis | vocāns, tenēns, dūcēns, capiēns, veniēns |
| perfect passive | 4th principal part | it IS the 4th part | vocātus, missus, captus, gestus |
| future active | 4th principal part | -um → -ūrus | vocātūrus, missūrus, captūrus, futūrus |

## How they decline

- **Perfect passive & future active:** 2-1-2, exactly like magnus: captus, capta,
  captum; captī, captae...
- **Present active:** 3rd declension, like ingēns: capiēns (all genders nom.),
  capientis, capientī, capientem, capientī/e; pl. capientēs/capientia, capientium...
  (Ablative singular: -ī when used as pure adjective, -e when verbal — recognition
  only; readings will make sense either way.)

## Tense is RELATIVE to the main verb

| Participle | Time relative to main verb |
|---|---|
| present (capiēns) | same time |
| perfect (captus) | before |
| future (captūrus) | after |

`mīlitem captum videō` — the capture happened BEFORE I see him.
`mīlitem captum vīdī` — the capture had happened before I saw him (both in my past).
Same participle; the main verb moves the reference point.

## The week-2 payoff: deponent participles

| Verb | Perfect participle | Meaning (ACTIVE!) |
|---|---|---|
| sequor | secūtus | having followed |
| loquor | locūtus | having spoken |
| proficīscor | profectus | having set out |
| patior | passus | having suffered/allowed |
| morior | mortuus | having died |
| ūtor | ūsus | having used |

`Sulla, Rōmam ingressus, inimīcōs occīdit.` — "Sulla, having entered Rome, killed his
enemies." One word (ingressus) does what English needs a clause for.
