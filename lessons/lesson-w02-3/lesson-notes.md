# Lesson-w02-3

## Primer

The diagnostic revealed something unusual: you already own the perfect-system passive ‚ÄĒ
you produced `capta est / erat / erit` unprompted, as a system. What was never tested
is the *present-system* passive: amatur, amabatur, amabitur. Today completes the
passive. The deep structure: Latin has two completely different passive machines.
Present system = active endings swapped for -r endings (one word). Perfect system =
participle + esse (two words ‚ÄĒ which you know). After today, every indicative verb
form in Latin, active or passive, is readable.

## Lesson Plan

Target 30 minutes. One concept: the -r passive completes the voice system.

1. **Hook (4 min).** `Exercitus novo modo paratur.` (from this week's reading w02-3 ‚ÄĒ
   or fresh if he hasn't read it yet: `Consul a populo creatur.`) What's being done,
   and by whom is it NOT being done ‚ÄĒ where did the subject's agency go? Passive as
   perspective flip.
2. **The -r endings (10 min).** -r, -ris, -tur, -mur, -minńę, -ntur mapped onto stems
   he owns: amŇć‚Üíamor, amńĀs‚ÜíamńĀris, amat‚ÜíamńĀtur. Then imperfect (amńĀbńĀtur) and future
   (amńĀbitur / dŇęcńďtur ‚ÄĒ watch 3rd conj. dŇęceris vs dŇęcńďris only differs by length:
   flag, move on). Drill: he converts six active forms to passive.
3. **Two machines, one voice (10 min).** Contrast table: `urbs capitur` (is being
   taken) / `urbs capta est` (was taken, is in a taken state). Have HIM articulate the
   aspect difference ‚ÄĒ he did it for active in the diagnostic; extending it to passive
   cements both. Agent (ńĀ/ab) and means (bare abl.) recur from w01-4: `a Mario
   parńĀtur` / `ferrŇć dńďlńďtur`.
4. **Wrap (6 min).** Parse mixed set (active/passive, both systems). Homework preview
   (~30 min). Tomorrow: verbs that are passive in form but active in meaning ‚ÄĒ the
   deponents ‚ÄĒ and why the -r endings were worth learning well.
