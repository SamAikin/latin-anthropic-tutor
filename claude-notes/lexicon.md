# Lexicon — agreed neologisms

Coined terms for things the Latin of our focus period (100 BCE – 100 CE) had no word
for. These are OUR shared vocabulary, not attested Latin — always flag them as coinages
if they appear in materials. Dictionary entries only; keep it light.

Format: headword, genitive, gender, declension — gloss. Then rationale and usage notes,
one or two lines each.

---

**ārida intellegentia, -ae, f.** (1st decl.) — *artificial intelligence.*
Lit. "dry intelligence." Coined by Sam, 2026-07-19.
- Preserves the English abbreviation **AI**.
- Plays on the sci-fi *wetware / dryware* opposition.
- **Usage: ablative of AGENT, not means** — `ab āridā intellegentiā`, never bare
  `āridā intellegentiā`. Adopted per the 2026-07-19 debate on whether Romans would
  have classed an AI as doer or instrument. Sam argued agent; I argued the Roman
  reflex would have been `per` + acc. (the intermediary). His ruling stands as our
  convention, and the coinage encodes it.
- Spelling note: classical orthography is *intellegentia* (from *intellegō*);
  *intelligentia* is late/medieval. Using the classical form.
- Adjective declines with it: `ārida, -ae`. Pl. `āridae intellegentiae`.
