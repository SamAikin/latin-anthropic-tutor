## Baseline

- Self-reported level: **rusty school Latin** — studied years ago; concepts expected to
  resurface quickly with review. His own words: "my grammar is quite rusty." Unknowns to
  probe in the first plan-syllabus placement diagnostic: how much morphology recall
  survives (declensions? subjunctive?), vocab retention, whether grammar metalanguage
  (ablative absolute, deponent, etc.) is familiar.
- First-block calibration directive (user, 2026-07-11): treat him like a beginner and let
  him SPEED RUN the first 4 weeks — under-shoot difficulty, brisk pace. He'd rather
  re-cover ground fast than stall in front of a hill he can't climb. He expects the first
  syllabus to be the poorest fit; after block one the 4-week cycle should self-correct.
- Ejection seat: the not-working skill (created 2026-07-11) handles mid-block non-fit
  (too hard, or far too easy) — rolls up progress, diagnoses, triggers plan-syllabus.
- Ultimate goal: deep fluency in late-republic Latin; acquisition of the language AND study
  of the late republic *through* the language (the forever North Star).
- Taste: Cicero-inspired. Respects Caesar as historically essential but does not want his
  viewpoint to predominate reading selection.
- Taste refinement (user, 2026-07-11, on approving first syllabus): late republic is the
  target *dialect*, not a fence. He wants a WELL-ROUNDED experience of the language:
  earlier-republic authors and late-empire authors are welcome from time to time, as
  lessons lend themselves. Cicero is the "elder statesman target" but most readings
  should NOT be Cicero's. What he wants to see: "the vista of the Roman experiment" —
  its most enlightened and democratic inventions AND its darkest lessons on the banality
  of systemic evil, as a cautionary tale. Curate readings toward that vista.
- Commitment: 70 vocab words/week (10/day) via Anki SRS; frequency-weighted word selection
  is critical to him ("ground speed toward fluency"). He hand-types his own Anki cards
  from vocab.md deliberately — it's his first encoding pass. Never generate card exports.
- Cadence: 4 lessons/week (~30 min target each, one concept per lesson, may flex when
  struggling), 6 daily readings/week (~30 min each), homework 15–45 min/lesson. All-in
  weekly commitment ≈ 9 hrs including daily Anki. He chose 30-min lessons over 45 —
  size lesson plans accordingly; don't overstuff.
- Style contract: no macrons / no vocab handles / no annotations in readings and homework;
  macrons and reference supplements OK in vocab lists and lessons.
- Reading purpose (user, 2026-07-11, post-assessment tweak): daily reading is READING
  practice, not translation practice — the learner reads for in-situ comprehension in
  Latin; the translation is only assessment evidence of that comprehension. Evaluate
  comprehension of the Latin, not the polish of the English.
- Model convention update (user, 2026-07-11, post-assessment tweak): grading homework and
  evaluating reading translations use the bigger (Opus-class) model, same as planning —
  only lesson execution stays on Sonnet. Rationale: a confidently wrong correction
  teaches the error, and a novice can't be relied on to catch it.
