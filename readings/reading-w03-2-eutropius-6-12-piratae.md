## Provenance and Citations

**ADAPTED HISTORICAL TEXT.** Source: Eutropius, *Breviarium ab urbe condita* 6.12,
verified against the Dickinson College Commentaries edition (dcc.dickinson.edu,
retrieved 2026-07-11). Eutropius wrote in the 4th century AD — five centuries after
these events — as a summarizer of Roman history for busy officials; his Latin is not
late-republic prose, but it is simple, classicizing, and the traditional first "real
text" for learners. The events: Pompey's pirate command (67 BC) and the transfer to him
of the Mithridatic war (66 BC).

Changes from the original, made to match your current level: the result clause "ita ut
... sola navigatio tuta non esset" (subjunctive, not yet taught) is recast as two
indicative sentences; the ablative absolute "quo suscepto" is unpacked into a plain
sentence; "delatum" is expanded to "delatum est"; "eius" and "hominum" added for
clarity. Everything else, including word choice, is Eutropius.

## Reading

Dum haec geruntur, piratae omnia maria infestabant. Romani toto orbe victores erant,
sed sola navigatio tuta non erat. Quare id bellum Cn. Pompeio decretum est, quod intra
paucos menses ingenti et felicitate et celeritate confecit.

Mox ei delatum est etiam bellum contra regem Mithridaten et Tigranen. Pompeius
Mithridaten in Armenia Minore nocturno proelio vicit, castra eius diripuit, quadraginta
milia hominum occidit.
