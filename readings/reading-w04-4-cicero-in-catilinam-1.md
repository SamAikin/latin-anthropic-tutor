# Daily Reading — w04-4

## Provenance and Citations

**REWORKED HISTORICAL TEXT.** Source: Cicero, *In Catilinam* 1.1–2, verified against
The Latin Library text (thelatinlibrary.com, retrieved 2026-07-11). Delivered in the
senate, in the temple of Jupiter Stator, on 8 November 63 BC, with Catiline himself
sitting in the room. This is the elder statesman's voice you've been aiming at — your
first taste of it, deliberately a stretch.

This is the most heavily reworked adaptation so far; treat it as Cicero's opening
*at reduced strength*, not as the text itself. Changes: the anaphora of "nihil" in the
second sentence is trimmed from six items to four; the indirect questions of the third
sentence ("quid proxima nocte egeris..." — subjunctive, block 2 grammar) are recast as
direct questions plus one indirect statement; "constrictam iam horum omnium scientia
teneri coniurationem tuam" is simplified; "si... vitemus" (subjunctive) becomes
indicative; the final sentence keeps Cicero's accusative-plus-infinitive with
"oportebat" intact. The famous lines — the opening three questions, "o tempora, o
mores," "consul videt; hic tamen vivit" — are verbatim or nearly so. Note "abutere" =
"abuteris" (future of the deponent abutor, alternate 2nd-person form; you will meet
this form constantly in real texts).

## Reading

Quo usque tandem abutere, Catilina, patientia nostra? quam diu etiam furor iste tuus
nos eludet? quem ad finem sese effrenata iactabit audacia?

Nihilne te nocturnum praesidium Palati, nihil urbis vigiliae, nihil timor populi, nihil
horum ora voltusque moverunt? Patere tua consilia non sentis? Coniurationem tuam
teneri iam ab omnibus non vides? Ubi proxima nocte fuisti? Quos convocavisti? Quid
consilii cepisti? Putasne quemquam nostrum haec ignorare?

O tempora, o mores! Senatus haec intellegit, consul videt; hic tamen vivit. Vivit? immo
vero etiam in senatum venit, fit publici consilii particeps, notat et designat oculis ad
caedem unum quemque nostrum. Nos autem, fortes viri, satis facere rei publicae videmur,
si istius furorem ac tela vitamus.

Ad mortem te, Catilina, duci iussu consulis iam pridem oportebat: in te conferri pestem
quam tu in nos omnes iam diu machinaris.
