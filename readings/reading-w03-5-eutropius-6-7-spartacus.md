# Daily Reading — w03-5

## Provenance and Citations

**ADAPTED HISTORICAL TEXT.** Source: Eutropius, *Breviarium ab urbe condita* 6.7 (the
Spartacus war), verified against the Dickinson College Commentaries edition
(dcc.dickinson.edu, retrieved 2026-07-11). See reading w03-2 for who Eutropius was.
This is the real ancient account behind reading w03-3.

Changes from the original: the chapter's opening sentence (M. Licinius Lucullus
receiving Macedonia) is omitted as unrelated; the long second sentence is split in two;
"in ea, quam Hannibal moverat" is smoothed to "quam illud quod Hannibal moverat"; word
order in the final clause is normalized and "inpositus" is spelled "impositus". The
ablative absolutes — "effracto Capuae ludo" and "multis ducibus et duobus simul
Romanorum consulibus victis" — are Eutropius's own and are deliberately retained: they
are exactly this week's grammar, in the wild.

## Reading

In Italia novum bellum subito commotum est. Septuaginta enim et quattuor gladiatores,
ducibus Spartaco, Crixo et Oenomao, effracto Capuae ludo fugerunt. Per Italiam vagantes
paene non levius bellum paraverunt quam illud quod Hannibal moverat.

Nam multis ducibus et duobus simul Romanorum consulibus victis, sexaginta fere milium
armatorum exercitum congregaverunt. Victi sunt autem in Apulia a M. Licinio Crasso pro
consule, et post multas calamitates Italiae tertio anno bello huic finis impositus est.
